VoIP & Telephony

Most L1 guides ignore phones — but this is Sempervox's business. Understand how a call actually flows and you'll stand out fast. Almost every VoIP fault comes back to the network.

🔌How VoIP actually works▾

VoIP (Voice over IP) turns your voice into data packets and sends them over the internet instead of a traditional phone line. The building blocks:

  • PBX — the phone system that routes calls for the business. A hosted/cloud PBX lives in the provider's cloud (this is the modern MSP model).
  • Extension — an internal number for a user or desk.
  • SIP trunk — the virtual line connecting the PBX to the public phone network, carrying multiple calls at once.
  • DID — a public phone number that rings a specific person/extension directly.
  • Handset / softphone — a physical desk phone, or an app on a PC/mobile.
📶Anatomy of a call: SIP vs RTP▾

This one distinction explains a huge share of VoIP tickets:

  • SIP (signalling) sets up, rings, and ends the call — the "dialling and connecting" part.
  • RTP (media) carries the actual audio once connected.

A phone registers to the PBX (SIP), then when a call connects the voice flows as RTP.

The key insight: if a call connects and rings but you get one-way or no audio, SIP is fine and RTP is being blocked — nearly always a firewall or NAT issue on the network. That single deduction impresses interviewers and seniors alike.
🔀Call routing▾

How an inbound call finds the right person:

  • Auto-attendant / IVR — "press 1 for sales" menus.
  • Ring group / hunt group — rings several phones together or in sequence.
  • Call queue — holds callers and feeds them to available agents.
  • Voicemail — catches unanswered calls; can email the recording.
  • Out-of-hours / time-based routing — different behaviour outside business hours or on holidays.

Common request: "calls aren't reaching X" — usually a routing/ring-group membership change, not a fault.

🎧Common VoIP tickets▾

Handset won't register / "no service"

Check network cable/PoE and power, that it has an IP, and that credentials/config are correct. A reboot re-registers it.

One-way audio or no audio

Call connects but no voice = RTP blocked → firewall/NAT. Check QoS and firewall rules; test on another network.

Poor quality — choppy, robotic, breaking up

Network problem: jitter, latency or packet loss. Check for congestion, missing QoS, or Wi-Fi (voice hates Wi-Fi — prefer wired).

Dropped calls

Intermittent connectivity, SIP timers, or an unstable internet link. Check the WAN and any recent network changes.

Echo

Often a hardware/headset or volume-level issue; sometimes a gateway config.

📊Quality & the network▾

Voice is real-time — packets that arrive late are useless, so VoIP is far more network-sensitive than email or web. What to know:

  • Bandwidth — a call uses roughly ~85–100 kbps each way; it's low, but it must be consistent.
  • Latency — keep under ~150 ms or people talk over each other.
  • Jitter — variation in packet timing; causes choppy audio.
  • Packet loss — even 1–2% is audible as gaps/robotic voice.
  • QoS — prioritises voice over other traffic so a big download doesn't wreck a call.
Golden rule: when in doubt on a VoIP fault, suspect the network — wired vs Wi-Fi, congestion, QoS, and firewall/NAT are the usual culprits.
🏢The platforms Sempervox uses▾

Sempervox delivers telephony on two platforms — so you'll meet both on tickets. The concepts above apply to each; here's where they actually differ.

1. Gamma Horizon (hosted phone system)

Gamma is a major UK telecoms wholesaler, and Horizon is their cloud-hosted PBX — no on-site phone hardware, hosted in resilient UK datacentres. It's managed through an easy web admin portal, and this is the one you'll log into most for phone tickets.

  • Three levels of control in the portal: company, site (location), and individual user — know which level a setting lives at.
  • Users can self-serve their own phone: call forwarding, voicemail, do-not-disturb.
  • Admins manage call groups, hunt groups, queues and auto-attendants (the "press 1 for sales" menus).
  • Apps — desktop and mobile softphones so users can work from anywhere.
  • Busy Lamp keys (see who's free), and Horizon Integrator for CRM screen-popping.
Typical L1 Horizon tickets: "change where my calls forward", "add someone to a ring group", "set up out-of-hours on the auto-attendant", "my softphone app won't sign in", "assign a new user a number/extension". Nearly all are done in the Horizon web portal.

2. Microsoft Teams Voice (Teams Phone)

Because Sempervox is a Microsoft house, some clients make and take external calls inside Teams rather than on a Horizon handset. This is managed in the Teams admin center (admin.teams.microsoft.com) and ties into the same MS365 identity you already know.

  • Users get a phone number assigned and a calling policy/licence in the Teams admin center.
  • External calling reaches the phone network via a calling plan, Operator Connect, or Direct Routing (how the numbers connect — often through Gamma).
  • Common tickets: "I can't dial external numbers from Teams", "no dial pad showing", "assign me a number" — usually a licence/policy or number-assignment issue.
First question on any phone ticket: "Are you on a Horizon phone/app, or calling through Teams?" That one answer tells you which portal to open — and stops you troubleshooting the wrong system.
